### **1. Cognitive Health Benefits**

**Reaction Time Training:**
- Button-press speed games measure cognitive processing
- Track decline/improvement over time
- Early dementia detection (slowing reactions)
- Mental stimulation prevents cognitive decline

**Memory Challenges:**
- Pattern recognition games
- Sequence memorization (Simon-style)
- Score tracking shows progress/decline

**Engagement:**
- Combats depression and isolation
- Social interaction during group games
- Sense of accomplishment and competition

---

### **2. Physical Therapy Applications**

**Motor Skills:**
- **Hand-eye coordination** - Button pressing, timing games
- **Fine motor control** - Gentle tapping, controlled movements
- **Grip strength** - Holding and manipulating puck
- **Range of motion** - Gentle shaking, passing exercises

**Measurable Progress:**
- Therapists track improvement over weeks/months
- Objective data (not subjective assessments)
- Medicare billing requires measurable outcomes
- Data export for medical records

**Fall Prevention:**
- Reaction time correlates with fall risk
- Regular testing identifies high-risk residents
- Intervention programs based on scores
- Proven: Faster reactions = fewer falls

**Group Activities:**
- Multi-puck competitions (10-20 residents at once)
- Team games (roommates vs. roommates)

- Bingo replacement (more engaging!)

**Daily Engagement:**
- Morning cognitive warm-ups
- Afternoon social games
- Evening wind-down activities
- Scheduled tournaments (weekly leaderboards)

**Therapeutic Recreation:**

- Meets regulatory requirements for "meaningful activities"
- Non-stigmatizing (it's "fun" not "therapy")
- Appeals to competitive Baby Boomers
